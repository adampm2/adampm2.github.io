Frequently asked questions

Answers to what people usually ask before their first visit. Still curious about something? give us a call.

Do I need an appointment, or do you take walk-ins?

Walk-ins are welcome whenever there's an open chair, but appointments are preferred so you're not waiting — especially on Saturdays. Book online two weeks out, or call for a same-week slot if one's open.

What's the difference between gel and acrylic, and which should I get?

Gel is applied over your natural nail and cures under UV light — it looks glassy and natural, lasts about 2–3 weeks, and is a gentler option if you're trying to grow out your own nails. Acrylic (a full set) adds length and structure with a durable overlay, better if you want extra strength or length your natural nail can't hold on its own. See our full manicure & pedicure pricing for both.

How long does a gel manicure actually last?

Most clients get 2–3 weeks of chip-free wear from a gel manicure before it needs a soak-off or fresh coat. How long yours lasts depends on your nail growth and how hard you are on your hands day to day.

How often do I need a fill?

Every 2–3 weeks is standard for acrylic or builder gel fills, as your natural nail grows out from underneath. Waiting much longer than that puts extra stress on the nail bed, so we'll always tell you honestly if it's time.

How much does a full set or gel manicure cost?

A gel manicure starts at $40, and a full acrylic set starts at $55. Hand-painted nail art, chrome finishes, and other add-ons are priced separately per hand. Full breakdown is on our services & price list.

Do you sanitize your tools between clients?

Yes — every tool is cleaned and disinfected between every client, in line with West Virginia Board of Cosmetology standards. Metal implements are disinfected in an EPA-registered hospital-grade solution, and single-use items (files, buffers) are never reused.

Can I see examples of your nail art before booking?

Definitely — browse recent work in the gallery, filterable by shade if you already know the vibe you're after (wine & red, nude, gold & chrome, or dark & moody).
